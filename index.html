GDPR Compliance and Dental Data Security in Europe

Patient enquiry data is personal data under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR 2016/679) and the UK GDPR as retained by the Data Protection Act 2018. Any automated processing of dental patient data — including AI-driven triage, personalised messaging, and CRM integration — requires a documented lawful basis, transparent data subject communication, enforced retention limits, and compliance with cross-border transfer rules where applicable. Processa's infrastructure satisfies every one of these requirements by architectural default.

All patient data is processed on EU-region servers certified to ISO 27001. Data is encrypted at rest using AES-256 and in transit using TLS 1.3 throughout the full pipeline. Retention schedules are aligned with ICO and CNIL guidance for healthcare-adjacent data controllers. No patient data is shared with advertising platforms, sold to data brokers, or used for any purpose outside the defined scope of dental patient acquisition and retention communications. Every practice receives a Data Processing Agreement as standard, with full audit-ready documentation for inclusion in GDPR compliance records. For dental clinics operating across multiple European jurisdictions, Processa supports dual-compliance frameworks covering both EU GDPR and applicable national implementing legislation.
